About Us

Bill Crouse, Sr.

Andrew Cashner

I live with my wife and children in Rochester, New York, just west of the Genesee River on land that President George Washington guaranteed forever to the Seneca Nation in the Candandaigua Treaty of 1794.

I teach and research music at the University of Rochester, and I am a pianist, singer, composer, and ensemble director. I lead the worship and creative arts ministry at New Hope Free Methodist Church in Rochester.

I am the descendant, probably, of German and English ancestors who came to the United States to farm Native land in Pennsylvania, Ohio, and Kentucky. I grew up in Richmond, Indiana, and studied piano performance at Lawrence University in Wisconsin and New England Conservatory in Boston. I have worked as a church musician for many years in many types of Christian churches, and I studied sacred music and organ performance at the University of Notre Dame. I received the PhD in the history and theory of music from the University of Chicago in 2015.

My doctoral research and first publications all concerned music as religious devotion in the Spanish Empire. My research interests include the history and theology of Christian worship, colonial societies, popular song in religious devotion, musical representations of racial difference, computer science and the history of science, and music history pedagogy.
